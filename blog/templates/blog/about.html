I grew up in China. I have been interested in mathematics and physics since I was a child. After winning the first price in national math olympiad, I was admitted to Peking University. There, I spent one year at the College of Engineering, then changed major to physics. I started my graduate student era at Texas A&M Unversity from 2014. I work mainly on neutrino physics, fitting physics models to experimental data and predicting discovery limits for new experiments. You can find my works here.

While I studied physics, I built up interests in computer technology and data science. I have been studying relevant topics such as Machine Learning since 2016. My background in physics data and statistics helped me quickly adapt to the concepts in data science. I have also attended the OMSCS program at Georgia Tech from 2018 to further extend my knowel.

I am actively looking for a data scientist position. Please contact me if you are interested. Here is my resume.
